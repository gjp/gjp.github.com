Project: Galley

Galley is a relational data entry and management application I developed for Proco Marketing, Inc. It replaced and expanded upon an existing app, moving from a 1990s popup-heavy, post-and-refresh interface to AJAX.

Proco's software suite is arranged around a curated recipe database which can associate ingredients with USDA nutritional values. Galley can estimate per-serving nutrition for entire recipes. It also handles recipe scaling, conversion between metric and US units, ingredient pluralization, and ingredient substitution.

Nearly 600 new recipes were created with this app during the ALDI promotion. Data entry will probably never be fun, but I had a vested interest in making the UX as painless as possible: I often used the tools myself, very much eating my own dog food.

Gally provides an interface for associating sale items with recipe ingredients and recipes, providing an incentive for customers to build meals around discounted ingredients.

This single screen encompasses relationships between about ten different models through a combination of search, autocomplete, and rollovers.

Galley displays data in a variety of ways. Here a page of sale items is shown much as they would be displayed on a public-facing site.

One possible solution to a many-to-many interface problem: Ingredient categories are mapped to store aisles by first selecting an aisle and then clicking all applicable categories.
